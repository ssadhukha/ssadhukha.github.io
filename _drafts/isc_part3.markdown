> This might be obvious to folks but one way I find helpful to think about ISC variants is to start with the question: what is the change in the signal that we care about? Do we care about how the signal changes over *time* (e.g. the time-varying signal)? Do we care about how the signal changes over *space*? Or both, across time *and* space? Thinking about it like this gives you a quick sense of what exactly goes into the vectors that you end up correlating.
